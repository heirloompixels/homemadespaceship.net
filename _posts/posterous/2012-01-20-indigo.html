A long anticipated project came to fruition today.  Indigo dyeing!  

The dye bath was mixed, and the 'flower' formed on top, the reaction of indigo dye with oxygen.  The yarn was dipped, soaked, removed and hung on the clothed line to dry.  When it first emerges from the dye vat, the yarn/fabric is green, only when it is exposed to oxygen during the drying process that it turns to it's classic indigo-blue color.
Kyle was so excited to be dyeing with indigo, he threw his shorts into the pot too. 
We did not expect the color to be so vibrant - blue it sure is.  Can't wait to start teaming up indigo dye with sage brush to make a nice yellow-green as well as cochineal and madder for some purples.  The possibilities are really quite endless!
